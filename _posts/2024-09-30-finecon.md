### Regression Analysis
Regression is touted as the most reliable tool at econometricians' disposal. Regression analysis is concerned with the study of the dependence of one variable, the dependent variable, on one or more other variables, the explanatory variables, to estimate and/or predict the (population) mean or average value of the former in terms of the known or fixed (in repeated sampling) values of the latter, see `Gujarati, 2003`.

Technically, regression is a statistical method that allows us to summarize and study relationships between two or more continuous (quantitative) variables:
+ One or k variable(s), denoted 𝑥_1, 𝑥_2, … 𝑥_𝑘, is regarded as the predictor, explanatory, regressor, or independent variable.
+ The other variable, denoted  𝑦, is the response, outcome, regressand, or dependent variable.
Regression aims at studying and predicting the mean value (thus random or stochastics) of a dependent variable 𝑦 regarding the base of a fixed value of 𝑥.
